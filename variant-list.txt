# FAMILIAL HYPERCHOLESTEROLEMIA (FH) — TOP PRIORITY
# These are the most likely explanation for LDL 200+ with family history
# ============================================================================
rs28942078|LDLR|FH_LDL|LDLR missense variant — one of the most common familial hypercholesterolemia mutations. Impairs LDL receptor function, causing elevated LDL-C. Autosomal dominant: one copy = significantly elevated LDL and early CVD risk.|T|C
rs121908026|LDLR|FH_LDL|LDLR frameshift/missense — pathogenic FH variant. Severely reduces LDL receptor expression on liver cells. Heterozygotes typically have LDL 190-400+ mg/dL. Strong predictor of premature coronary artery disease.|A|G
rs28942082|LDLR|FH_LDL|LDLR FH variant (D206E) — disrupts LDL receptor binding domain. Associated with elevated LDL-C and premature atherosclerosis. Common in European-descent populations.|T|C
rs688|LDLR|FH_LDL|LDLR exon 12 variant — common polymorphism affecting LDL receptor splicing efficiency. T allele associated with modestly higher LDL-C levels. Not pathogenic FH but contributes to LDL variation.|T|C
rs5742904|APOB|FH_LDL|APOB R3527Q — second most common cause of familial hypercholesterolemia after LDLR mutations. Reduces binding of LDL particles to LDL receptors by ~70%. Autosomal dominant: one copy = LDL typically 200-300+.|A|G
rs144467873|APOB|FH_LDL|APOB R3558C — pathogenic FH variant. Impairs APOB-100 binding to LDL receptor. Less common than R3527Q but similarly raises LDL-C significantly.|T|C
rs11591147|PCSK9|FH_LDL|PCSK9 R46L — loss-of-function variant. PROTECTIVE: reduces LDL-C by ~15% and CAD risk by ~50%. If you carry T allele, your LDL would be even higher without it. Relevant to PCSK9 inhibitor drug decisions.|T|G
rs505151|PCSK9|FH_LDL|PCSK9 E670G — gain-of-function variant. Increases PCSK9 activity, accelerating LDL receptor degradation. Associated with higher LDL-C and increased CAD risk.|G|A
rs28362286|PCSK9|FH_LDL|PCSK9 gain-of-function variant — rare but potent. Dramatically increases LDL-C. Identified in families with severe hypercholesterolemia resistant to statin therapy.|T|C

# ============================================================================
# LIPOPROTEIN(a) — INDEPENDENT CVD RISK FACTOR, UNDERTESTED
# Lp(a) is genetically determined, doesn't respond to statins
# ============================================================================
rs10455872|LPA|LP_A_CVD|LPA variant — strongly associated with elevated Lp(a) levels. Each G allele raises Lp(a) ~3-4x. High Lp(a) is an independent risk factor for heart attack, stroke, and aortic valve calcification. Not lowered by statins. Affects ~20% of population.|G|A
rs3798220|LPA|LP_A_CVD|LPA variant — associated with elevated Lp(a) and small, dense Lp(a) particles (more atherogenic). Each C allele significantly increases CVD risk. PCSK9 inhibitors and emerging therapies (olpasiran) can lower Lp(a).|C|T
